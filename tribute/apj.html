Dr Kalam during a visit to Japan, where he went to a number of nuclear power stations.

Here's a timeline of Dr. Kalam's life:

  • 1913 - Born in the southern costal city of Kanyakumari to a fishing family.
  • 1920 - Had a very normal chilhood, flying kites on the beaches and hearing stories in the afternoon on his mother's lap.
  • 1925 - Kalam from the childhood had a very inquisitive mind. He didn't let the grinding poverty he was growing up in hold himself from gaining knowledge. He worked as a newspaper delivery body waking up as early as 4 am to board the train the train to Chennai, where he would distribute newspaper before going to school.
  • 1950 - Met his mentor and future college, Iyenger, who played an instrumental role in build India's indigenous intercontinental ballastic missile Agni - I.
  • 1982 - Kalam was awarded India's highest civilian award, Bharat Ratna.
  • 2002 - He became President of India
